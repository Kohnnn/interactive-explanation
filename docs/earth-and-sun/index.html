Source snapshot

The local route vendors the published article HTML, shared article CSS, page-local CSS, route-local globe textures, and scripts, then patches only the subpath-hosting shell and provenance surfaces needed for the public replica.

  • Live original verified against https://ciechanow.ski/earth-and-sun/ on 2026-03-21.
  • The local copy removes the upstream banner, blog navigation, analytics, Patreon prompt, and social links from the public body.
  • The orbit, day-length, axial-tilt, precession, and seasonal scenes remain driven by the vendored scripts rather than a local rewrite.

Asset handoff

  • earth-and-sun/index.html owns the local metadata shell, shared footer hook, and relative article asset paths.
  • earth-and-sun/css/base.css and earth-and-sun/css/earth_sun.css preserve the published article layout and interaction styling with subpath-safe helper image URLs.
  • earth-and-sun/js/base.js and earth-and-sun/js/earth_sun.js keep the original globe, orbit, slider, and play-pause runtime.
  • earth-and-sun/images/earth_sun/ contains the route-local Earth textures, border sprites, and outline asset requested by the published runtime.

Known deviations

  • The public route no longer includes the author banner, Patreon prompt, X/Twitter, Instagram, e-mail, RSS, or Google Analytics surfaces.
  • The local copy uses local fallback font stacks instead of runtime Google Fonts requests so the route stays self-contained.
  • Later Bartosz routes can now rewrite local `earth-and-sun` references directly instead of neutralizing them in the public body.

Validation guidance

  • Keep the article structure intact; future edits should patch the published shell, not rewrite the interactions into a condensed local experience.
  • Extend the shared smoke bundle through the Ciechanowski family helpers instead of creating a one-off validator.
  • Run both node interactive-explanation/tools/check-public-surface.mjs and node interactive-explanation/tools/smoke-bundle.mjs after touching article shell copy or any vendored runtime files.
