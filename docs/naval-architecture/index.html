Source snapshot

The local route vendors the published article HTML, shared article CSS, page-local CSS, route-local naval model data, and scripts, then patches only the subpath-hosting shell and provenance surfaces needed for the public replica.

  • Live original verified against https://ciechanow.ski/naval-architecture/ on 2026-03-21.
  • The local copy removes the upstream banner, blog navigation, analytics, Patreon prompt, and social links from the public body.
  • The pressure, buoyancy, hull, cargo, free-surface, wave, and propeller sections remain driven by the vendored scripts rather than a local rewrite.

Asset handoff

  • naval-architecture/index.html owns the local metadata shell, shared footer hook, and relative article asset paths.
  • naval-architecture/css/base.css and naval-architecture/css/navarch.css preserve the published article layout and interaction styling with subpath-safe helper image URLs.
  • naval-architecture/js/base.js and naval-architecture/js/navarch.js keep the original drag, slider, and animation runtime.
  • naval-architecture/models/ contains the route-local geometry blobs requested by the published ship and propeller scenes.

Known deviations

  • The public route no longer includes the author banner, Patreon prompt, X/Twitter, Instagram, e-mail, RSS, or Google Analytics surfaces.
  • The local copy uses local fallback font stacks instead of runtime Google Fonts requests so the route stays self-contained.
  • In-body Ciechanowski article links are rewritten locally when available or neutralized when no local replica exists yet.

Validation guidance

  • Keep the article structure intact; future edits should patch the published shell, not rewrite the interactions into a condensed local experience.
  • Extend the shared smoke bundle through the Ciechanowski family helpers instead of creating a one-off validator.
  • Run both node interactive-explanation/tools/check-public-surface.mjs and node interactive-explanation/tools/smoke-bundle.mjs after touching article shell copy or any vendored runtime files.
